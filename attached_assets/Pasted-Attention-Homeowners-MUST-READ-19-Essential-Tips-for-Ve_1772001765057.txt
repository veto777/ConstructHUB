The Problem with Lack of Permitting in Skagit and Whatcom Counties
One of the biggest challenges homeowners face when hiring contractors in Skagit and Whatcom counties is the complete lack of permitting requirements for siding, window, and door installations. Unlike major cities such as Seattle, where these types of projects require permits and inspections, these counties have no oversight in place. This creates a massive loophole that allows unqualified and unlicensed contractors to perform work without proper experience or accountability.

The Dangers of No Permitting & No Inspections
Without required permits, the risks to homeowners increase significantly:

Unqualified Contractors: Many companies operating in these counties either hire unlicensed workers or are lead generation firms that hold a business license but subcontract all the work to inexperienced or unqualified installers.
No Oversight = Poor Workmanship: Since no inspections are required, contractors can cut corners without fear of consequences. Improperly installed siding, windows, and doors can lead to structural damage, water infiltration, and costly repairs down the road.
Deceptive Business Practices: Many of these companies present themselves as large, reputable businesses by investing in sleek websites, branded trucks, and professional marketing. However, these appearances can be deceiving—what you may actually be dealing with is a lead generation company that outsources your project to the lowest bidder.
Why Experience Matters More Than Ever :

Because permitting and inspections serve as a safeguard against poor workmanship, the lack of these protections makes it crucial to verify a contractor’s experience. A company that has been in business for less than 10 years is more likely to be cutting corners and making costly installation mistakes.

How to Protect Yourself:

Check Their Licensing & Track Record: Ensure the company you hire has a verifiable history of successful projects in your area. Always ask for active jobs in the area and see if they have any active jobs. An experienced and successful company will always have an active job in your area – even in a slow season. 
Look Beyond the Marketing: A flashy website and a fleet of branded trucks/cars don’t equal experience—ask for references and proof of work.

Demand Proper Installation Standards: Even if permits aren’t required, insist on industry-standard installation practices and third-party inspections if necessary.

Be Wary of “Too-Good-to-Be-True” Prices: Low bids often indicate a company is cutting costs by hiring unskilled labor or they are undercutting the market because of no overhead (no expenses).
Permits and inspections are game changers when it comes to protecting your home from shoddy workmanship. In areas where they aren’t required, it’s up to homeowners to be extra diligent in choosing a qualified contractor. Don’t fall for illusions—do your homework and hire a company with a proven track record of excellence.

In conclusion, these practices not only violate ethical standards but also harm both customers and the business itself in the long run. It’s essential for businesses to be truthful and transparent about their qualifications and experience to maintain trust and integrity in their relationships with customers.

3. The Problem with Vanishing Estimates

We recently discovered a troubling trend among shady contractors: vanishing estimates—quotes that disappear after a customer reviews them. These companies create estimates, but when you go back to reference them, they’re mysteriously deleted. This is not just deceptive—it’s a massive red flag.

Why Do Companies Delete Estimates?
Some businesses claim they remove estimates to “protect their pricing” or prevent customers from sharing them. In reality, the only thing they’re protecting is themselves—from accountability, liability, and transparency.

The Dangers of Vanishing Estimates
No Record, No Proof: If a company can erase an estimate at will, what’s stopping them from altering or even deleting a signed contract? This opens the door to price changes, hidden fees, and disputes.
Lack of Accountability: A reputable business stands by its quotes. Removing estimates suggests they’re afraid of being held to their original pricing or scope of work.
Customer Manipulation: If you can’t reference an estimate later, the company has total control over the conversation. They can claim you “misunderstood” the pricing or that your project was never quoted as you remember.
No Long-Term Transparency: We provide estimates that customers can review years later because we stand by our pricing, workmanship, and commitment to honesty. If a company sends you a link that later leads to nowhere, it’s a clear sign they’re not operating in good faith.
A Company That Deletes an Estimate Can Delete a Contract
If a company can make your estimate disappear, what’s stopping them from altering or even erasing your signed contract? This kind of shady business practice puts homeowners at risk of unexpected charges, incomplete work, and legal headaches.

How to Protect Yourself
Always Request a Downloadable or Printed Copy of your estimate. If a company only provides a disappearing link, insist on a PDF or hard copy.

Read Reviews & Ask Questions about their process for handling estimates and contracts.

Choose a Contractor That Stands by Their Word. A company that deletes estimates is likely to cut corners elsewhere—don’t take the risk.

At the end of the day, a disappearing estimate is a sign of a disappearing commitment to honesty and quality work. Don’t trust a contractor that won’t stand behind their pricing

4. The Illusion of size:  Many smaller companies or lead generation entities go to great lengths to appear like large, established businesses. They often lack a real office or physical location, relying instead on multiple Google business pages to generate calls from various cities. These listings might use virtual addresses, such as post office boxes or storage units. They create impressive websites and saturate the internet to attract clients, and may even have branded work trucks or other vehicles to foster trust and give an impression of size. Typically, these businesses are run by single owners without any staff. These owners may conceal their role, posing as estimators, project managers, or salespeople, when in fact they are the sole operator of the business. This is often due to their young age or lack of experience. Don’t be deceived by these tactics. If something goes wrong, these companies often vanish with your money or fail to fix their mistakes, leaving you with zero accountability.

5. Lower Prices Do NOT Mean Equal Service:  Many of you have heard the phrase “comparing apples to apples,” but it’s rarely applied correctly. Comparing a 20-year-old company with a full staff and multiple locations to a 1-year-old company operating out of a pickup truck is not a fair comparison.

Established companies bring years of experience and advanced problem-solving capabilities that are often absent in newer firms. Their extensive background enables them to identify potential pitfalls in a project that inexperienced companies may overlook. For instance, a homeowner once hired a new construction company to expand a window. Midway through the project, a significant structural issue was discovered, one that an established firm would likely have anticipated and planned for from the start. This oversight led to costly delays and additional expenses that could have been avoided.

Furthermore, established companies typically carry comprehensive insurance, which safeguards both the project and the personnel involved. This protection is crucial, as it ensures that homeowners are not held liable for any incidents or injuries that may occur during the project. We know of a case where another company faced a serious issue: one of their employees fell off a ladder, and because the company did not carry the proper insurance for his protection, the employee ended up suing the homeowner. The lawsuit was successful, as the contractor was neither willing to cover the injury costs nor had workers’ compensation insurance. This situation underscores the importance of working with an established, properly insured company to avoid such liabilities.

Recently, we heard a cautionary tale about a customer who hired a less established company. The project, which was supposed to take a few weeks, stretched to six months. Worse, the company claimed to use Hardie products but didn’t, leading to a lawsuit. The customer discovered that the company’s insurance did not cover defective work or incorrect products. They could only recover a $6,000 bond which was carried by the contractor, which was insignificant compared to their legal expenses. The lesson here is clear: don’t always go for the cheapest bid. It’s often wiser to find a middle ground, ensuring you receive quality service without excessive hidden costs and potential lawsuits.

When you encounter a company or individual who criticizes a reputable business, often claiming things like “I used to work for them, and they treat employees terribly,” or “This company is trying to take us down because we’re growing,” it’s essential to view these statements with skepticism. The truth is, people who resort to these kinds of tactics typically lack strong ethical foundations. They might be trying to manipulate perceptions and create doubt around trusted names to make themselves look more appealing to potential customers or employees.

Moreover, these individuals or companies will often play the victim card, trying to cast themselves as the underdog or the wronged party, hoping to stir up emotions and gain sympathy. They know that by creating conflict and drama, they can manipulate opinions and draw attention to their own business, even if they don’t have the experience or track record to back it up.

It’s a red flag when a company actively works to tear down others rather than build up their own reputation with honest work and integrity. Always be cautious when a competitor or newcomer tries to tarnish the image of a long-standing business. Companies that resort to these dirty tactics likely don’t have the knowledge, skills, or good reputation to succeed based on merit alone.

For us in the industry, maintaining high ethical standards is critical. We should always be vigilant, question the motivations behind negative claims, and remember that reputation and honesty are the true cornerstones of long-term success.

7. Being licensed does NOT mean legitimacy or experience: Having a license doesn’t necessarily equate to expertise or reliability. In Washington state, acquiring a contractor’s license simply involves paying a fee, obtaining a bond, and securing insurance, totaling around $65 for licensing, $300 annually for a bond, and $100 monthly for general liability insurance. Unlike states like California or Hawaii, Washington doesn’t mandate prior experience. In California, for instance, a stringent process demands four years of experience, rigorous exams, credit and background checks before granting a license. In contrast, Washington doesn’t require testing or prior experience, leading to exploitation of this regulatory gap

11. Geographical Presence and Physical Office:

When selecting a contractor for your project, it’s crucial to take steps to validate their legitimacy and ensure you’re dealing with a credible business. One effective way to do this is by scheduling an appointment to meet the contractor in person at their physical office. This offers you a valuable opportunity to confirm their business presence and get a better sense of how they operate.

Start by asking if the contractor has a showroom or office where you can meet them face-to-face. A reputable company with a legitimate business will have a physical space where you can interact with their team, view their products or services, and discuss your project in detail. It’s also a good idea to inquire how many people are employed at the company. A company that’s well-established will typically have a staff to handle various aspects of the business, from customer service to project management. If a company is hesitant to answer these basic questions, it’s a sign that they might not be as reliable as they appear.

Location is another critical factor. Verify the geographic location of their office, as having a physical presence in a known location can lend credibility. Some companies try to hide behind virtual addresses or operate from residential properties or post office boxes. This is a common tactic used by lead generation companies or firms that don’t have a true office. They might list a P.O. box or a suite number in their address, which could be a red flag.

To be extra cautious, take the time to verify the office address. A quick search on Google Maps can help you confirm whether the business actually exists at the listed location. If you’re still unsure, consider doing a drive-by of the listed address to check for yourself. Many lead generation companies, for example, use random warehouses or storage units as a false address, hoping to trick customers into thinking they have an actual, professional office.

By taking these extra steps, you’re not just protecting yourself from potential scams or unreliable contractors—you’re ensuring that the business you choose is credible, experienced, and committed to delivering quality work. Your home is your most valuable asset, so it’s essential to work with a contractor that you can trust, and verifying their legitimacy is the first step in the right direction.

For customers navigating the digital landscape, it’s essential to discern between genuine construction companies committed to quality and service and these lead generation entities focused solely on driving traffic for profit.
